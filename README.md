# The U.S.-Israel War on Iran and Its Global Implications

## Project Overview
This repository contains a comprehensive geopolitical case study analyzing the 2026 conflict between the United States, Israel, and Iran. The study explores the historical roots of the conflict, the strategic military and asymmetrical responses, and the profound economic disruptions caused by the weaponization of maritime trade routes.

## Abstract
The 2026 war represents a culmination of decades of systematic political and economic pressure. This paper investigates the premise that the conflict is rooted in broader agendas of geopolitical domination rather than isolated security concerns. Key focus areas include:
* **Military Analysis:** Coordinated kinetic and cyber offensives (Operation Epic Fury).
* **Strategic Deterrence:** Iran's use of asymmetrical warfare and control of the Strait of Hormuz.
* **Global Economic Impact:** Detailed analysis of energy market volatility and agricultural crises in the Global South.
* **Diplomatic Deadlock:** The structural limitations of the United Nations and the rise of multipolar power structures (China, Russia, and India).

## Key Statistics & Findings
* **Energy Disruptions:** 20%–25% of global petroleum and 20% of LNG trade impacted by the Strait of Hormuz blockade.
* **Asian Market Exposure:** 84% of crude moving through the Strait is destined for Asian markets (China, India, Japan, South Korea).
* **Agricultural Crisis:** Fertilizer (Urea) prices surged over 140%, leading to a projected 18%–22% drop in crop yields in Sub-Saharan Africa.
* **Shipping Logistics:** Rerouting around the Cape of Good Hope increased transit times by 45% and costs by up to $50M per week.
